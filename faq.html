FAQ — questions asked & answers

A running log of questions the user has asked about Fire Emblem: Three Houses, with the answer recorded each time. Newest questions appended at the bottom. 用户提出的问题与解答记录（每次都登记答案）。

Q1. Do all classes need to be maxed (mastered)? If not, when do you switch?

Short answer / 简答: No — you do not need to master every class. Master a class only to harvest its permanent mastery ability (or a combat art) that you actually want; otherwise switch up as soon as a better class is available. 不需要把所有兵种都精通。只有当你想要某个兵种的精通技能（或战技）时才去精通它， 否则一旦能转到更好的兵种就转。

How class mastery works

  • Class level / mastery % is separate from character level. You fill a class's mastery bar by fighting and winning while in that class. Filling it teaches that class's mastery ability permanently (and sometimes a combat art).
  • Certification (switching classes) needs: the required skill ranks + a character level threshold, then passing the exam — Intermediate at Lv 10, Advanced at Lv 20, Master at Lv 30. Switching does not require mastering the class you're leaving.

So: master a class only if its ability/art is worth it

High-value mastery abilities worth a detour: Brigand → Death Blow (+6 Atk on initiate), Archer → Hit +20, Pegasus Knight → Darting Blow (+5 Spd on initiate), Mage → Fiendish Blow (+6 Mag on initiate). These are why guides say "master Brigand for Death Blow" even when Brigand isn't in your final class path — it's a mastery detour, not a destination (see the per-character files' "Mastery detours / skills to grab" sections).

When to switch vs. stay

  • Switch immediately when you hit a certification you want and you're not mid-mastering something useful — staying in a weak class too long costs you stats/abilities in battle.
  • Stay to master when the class has an ability/art you want (and you're close to filling the bar). Grab Death Blow / Hit +20 / Darting Blow / Fiendish Blow before moving on.
  • Don't bother mastering classes whose ability you won't use, filler classes you only pass through, or anything on Normal/easy runs where the extra power is unnecessary.
  • Endgame: once you're in your final Master class with the abilities you want, just stay — no need to keep mastering.

Rule of thumb / 经验法则: Plan your final class path first, then list the few mastery detours for the skills you want, grab those on the way, and skip mastering everything else. 先规划好最终转职路线，再列出想要技能的少数“精通绕路”，顺路拿到即可，其余无需精通。

Q2. For a class OFF your final path (e.g. master Brigand for Death Blow) — master it EARLY or LATE?

Short answer / 简答: Grab off-path mastery detours as EARLY as practical — the ability is permanent, so the sooner you own it the more battles it pays off in. The usual window is the Intermediate tier (~Lv 10–20), before you settle into your Advanced/Master final class. 把不在最终路线上的“精通绕路”尽量早拿——精通技能是永久的，越早到手收益的战斗越多； 通常在中级兵种阶段（约 10–20 级）、定型到高级/大师职前顺手吃满。

Why early

  • Mastery abilities (Death Blow, Hit +20, Darting Blow, Fiendish Blow…) are permanent and equippable in any later class. Owning Death Blow at Lv 12 buffs every fight from then on; owning it at Lv 35 wasted 20+ chapters of +6 Atk.
  • The early/intermediate detours are also the cheapest to fill — early classes master fast and early enemies are easy mastery fodder.
  • Exam timing isn't blocked: you can certify into your real class the moment you hit its level/skill gate even if a detour bar isn't full — but if you want the detour's ability, finish that bar before you leave, because mastery % stops accruing once you change class.

Practical ordering

  1. Beginner (Lv 1–9): play your natural early class; don't force detours yet.
  2. Intermediate (Lv 10–~19): this is the detour window — dip Brigand → Death Blow, Archer → Hit +20, Pegasus → Darting Blow, Mage → Fiendish Blow, Cavalier → Desperation as your build wants. Stack them here while exams are cheap.
  3. Advanced/Master (Lv 20/30+): settle into your final class and stay; only master the final class itself if its mastery ability/combat art is one you'll keep equipped.

Exceptions (master late, or skip)

  • Skip on Normal/easy runs — the extra power isn't needed (e.g. Azure Moon).
  • Late joiners (Gilbert, Cyril, Alois, Jeritza, Hanneman) often arrive past the cheap window — grab only the ONE detour that matters most (usually Death Blow / their faire-relevant skill) and don't backtrack for the rest.
  • Master-tier mastery abilities (Quick Riposte, Defiant Crit, etc.) are necessarily late — that's fine, they're your final class's reward, not a detour.

Q5. How many class masteries can one character EQUIP at once?

Short answer / 简答: Mastered abilities go into your 5 equippable skill slots, so a character can have up to 5 mastery / skill-level abilities equipped at the same time (they share those 5 slots with skill-rank abilities like Swordfaire). You can learn and permanently own as many masteries as you want over the game — you just can't have more than 5 equipped at once. 精通学到的技能永久拥有，但装备栏只有 5 个——同一时间最多装备 5 个精通/技能等级技能 （与剑术专精等共用这 5 格）。学多少不限，装备上限是 5。

The full ability budget (from the abilities data)

A character can have up to 9 abilities equipped at one time: - 1 Personal ability — innate, can't be removed (e.g. Byleth's Teacher's Guidance). - up to 3 Class abilities — granted by the current class; they change/disappear when you reclass (e.g. a flier's flying + Avo, a class's Faire). - up to 5 Skill-level / Mastery abilities ← this is the pool your mastered abilities use. Freely mixed and matched. Death Blow, Hit +20, Darting Blow, Fiendish Blow, Quick Riposte, the Faires, Alert Stance+, etc. all compete for these 5 slots.

Practical takeaway

  • Master as many useful abilities as you like — they're permanent, and you swap which 5 you equip per map/role.
  • That's why every per-character "Loadout" section in this project lists exactly 5 equipped skills — a typical optimized set is one Faire + Death Blow + Hit +20 + a Defiant/Avo skill + a build-defining mastery (e.g. Quick Riposte).
  • You do not need a mastery equipped to keep it — owning it is permanent; equipping is the per-slot choice.

Q6. Isn't it good to just have 5 masteries equipped, then?

Short answer / 简答: Not quite — the goal is the best 5 equipped skills, not 5 mastery abilities. Mastery abilities and skill-rank abilities share the same 5 slots, and some of the strongest skills in the game are skill-rank abilities (the Faires, Alert Stance+), not class masteries. A great loadout is usually a mix, and it's often only ~2–3 masteries + a Faire + a skill-rank skill. 目标是“最好的 5 个已装备技能”，不是“5 个精通技能”。 精通技能和技能等级技能共用这 5 格，而游戏里最强的一些技能（各种专精 Faire、警戒站姿+） 来自技能等级而非精通——所以最优配置通常是混搭，往往只有 2–3 个精通 + 1 个 Faire + 1 个技能等级技能。

Why "5 masteries" is the wrong target

  1. Faires beat most masteries and aren't masteries. Swordfaire/Lancefaire/Axefaire/ Bowfaire/Fistfaire/Black/Dark Tomefaire (+5 Mt with that weapon type) come from hitting A-rank in the skill, not from mastering a class. Almost every carry equips one — that's one of your 5 slots gone to a non-mastery.
  2. Alert Stance+ (huge Avo when you wait), Battalion Wrath/Vantage, White Magic Avo +20, the various +Mt/+Hit rank skills — also skill-rank, also slot-competitors.
  3. Diminishing returns / overlap. Many masteries are conditional or redundant: the whole Defiant ___ family only works below 25% HP; Death Blow vs Darting Blow vs Hit +20 all want the initiate turn; you rarely want three at once on the same unit.
  4. Class abilities already cover a lot. Your current class gives up to 3 free class abilities (flying, Canto, Avo, Barehanded Combat, etc.), so your 5 personal slots are for augmenting a build, not duplicating what the class already does.

What a good 5-slot loadout actually looks like

  • Physical carry (Wyvern/War Master): Faire (skill-rank) · Death Blow (mastery) · Hit +20 (mastery) · Alert Stance+ (skill-rank) · Quick Riposte / Defiant Crit (mastery) → ~3 masteries, 2 skill-rank.
  • Mage (Gremory/Warlock): Black Tomefaire (skill-rank) · Fiendish Blow (mastery) · Hit +20 (mastery) · White Magic Avo +20 (skill-rank) · Warding Blow / a breaker → mix.
  • Bow unit (Sniper/Bow Knight): Bowfaire · Hit +20 · Death Blow · Hunter's Volley (that's a combat art, not a slot skill) → Faire + masteries + Avo skill.

Bottom line / 总结: fill the 5 slots with the strongest skills for that unit's job — often a Faire + a couple of high-value masteries + a defensive/Avo skill. Chasing "5 masteries" specifically would exclude the Faires, which are usually your single best slot. Mix > mono.

Q7. Forums say the Noble / Commoner starting class is still worth mastering — agree?

Short answer / 简答: Usually no, with one real exception. The forums are pointing at the fact that mastering Noble or Commoner teaches a small mastery ability that boosts EXP gain (the early-class mastery bonus), which can help a unit catch up in levels faster. It is a niche EXP-grind convenience, not a combat skill — fine for a late/under-leveled joiner you're power-leveling, but skip it on units that are already on-curve, because filling the Noble/ Commoner bar wastes early chapters you'd rather spend mastering Brigand (Death Blow) etc. 论坛说的是：精通贵族/平民起始职会得到一个提升经验获取的精通技能，对练级追赶有用——属于 刷级便利而非战斗技能。落后的晚加入角色值得；已跟上进度的角色不值得（不如去精通土匪拿致命一击）。 ⚠️ verify: exact Noble/Commoner mastery ability name & numbers in-game (these starting classes aren't in this project's classes/ set).

When it IS worth it

  • A unit you're deliberately power-leveling (late joiners — Gilbert, Cyril, Alois, Hanneman, or any recruit who joins under-leveled): the EXP-gain mastery bonus speeds the catch-up, and these units often have spare early time anyway.
  • You're playing slowly / Normal and want everyone leveled without grinding.

When it's NOT worth it

  • On-curve carries: every chapter spent mastering Noble/Commoner is a chapter NOT spent grabbing Death Blow (Brigand) / Hit +20 (Archer) / Fiendish Blow (Mage) — those combat masteries are worth far more on a unit who's already keeping pace.
  • Maddening efficiency runs: EXP is tighter, but so is time; you almost always want the combat detours first and let levels come from kills, not from a mastery bonus.

Net

A reasonable middle path: if a unit is behind, let them finish their Noble/Commoner bar before you push them out of the starting class (you're sitting there at low level anyway) — you'll bank the EXP-gain skill for free. For everyone on pace, leave the starting class as soon as you hit Brigand/Archer/Mage so you can grab the combat masteries that actually win fights. Worth-it verdict overall: ★ situational (EXP catch-up only), not ★★/★★★.

Q7. Do I need to buy flowers for everyone's birthday?

Short answer / 简答: No. Buying a character flowers on their birthday is optional and gives only a small, fixed support bump (you can't choose the flower; small chance of a little motivation). Skip it for units you won't use; for your active roster / recruit targets it's a cheap minor boost worth grabbing only if convenient. 不需要。生日送花是可选的，只给小幅且固定的支持度提升 （不能选花种），对不用的角色可跳过；对在用或想招募的角色，顺手送一下即可。

This is about other characters' birthdays. For Byleth's own birthday gift (the Charm +2 accessory each part), see Q4.

Better ways to build support (higher ROI than birthday flowers)

  • Liked gifts — every character has gift preferences; giving one they like is a larger support bump (+ a chance at motivation). Buy gift items from the merchant and hand them out.
  • Greenhouse favorite flowers — cultivate flowers in the greenhouse and give each character their favorite flower for a bigger gain than the generic birthday flower. This is the flower method actually worth doing.
  • Talk to a unit on their birthday during exploration — a small free support bump regardless of flowers; do that.

Bottom line / 总结: don't buy birthday flowers for everyone. Reserve gift effort for your planned roster and recruit targets, and prefer liked gifts + favorite greenhouse flowers over the generic birthday flower. 别给所有人买生日花；把礼物精力留给计划阵容与招募对象，优先用喜爱的礼物 + 温室最爱之花。

Q8. Training priority: focus the ultimate-path skills first, or the detour-class skill (e.g. Axe to C for Death Blow)?

Short answer / 简答: Make your final-path primary skill the main, all-game focus — it's the long grind (needs A / A+ / S for the Faire and combat arts) and defines the unit. Treat detour skills as a small, early side-investment: they only need a C rank to certify (Brigand = Axe C, Archer = Bow C, Mage = Reason C, all + Lv 10), so nudge them to C during the intermediate window, master the class for the permanent ability, then stop. They barely compete with the main skill. 以最终路线主修技能为全程重点（要练到 A/A+/S，最耗时）；“精通绕路”技能只需 C 即可认证（土匪=斧 C、弓兵=弓 C、 魔法师=理学 C，皆需 10 级），中级阶段顺手点到 C、精通后即停。

Why they don't really conflict

  • A detour needs only C; your final skill needs A→S. The detour is a one-time cheap dip; the main skill is the long haul. Pour the bulk of instruction into the main skill and let the detour crawl to C.
  • Timing (see Q2): grab detours in the Intermediate window (Lv 10–20), while the main skill keeps climbing. Early game = main skill + nudge the detour to C; then master the detour class and commit fully to the final path.

Let the character's BOONS decide the detour

  • Detour skill is a boon / neutral → grab it (boons rank up faster; cheap to hit C).
  • Detour skill is a bane (weakness) → even C is slow; skip that detour, or grind it via Seminars / auxiliary battles so it doesn't steal manual instruction from your main skill.
  • Best case — overlap: if the detour skill is your final weapon, there's no conflict. e.g. an Axe Wyvern Lord grabbing Death Blow via Brigand trains Axe for both. A pure mage should take Mage → Fiendish Blow (Reason C, on-type) and not train off-type Axe just for Death Blow.

Practical per-unit order

  1. Final-path primary skill → main instruction focus, all game (to A/A+/S).
  2. Detour cert skill(s) → just to C, early, preferably a boon; master the class; stop.
  3. Secondary class skills (Flying / Riding / Authority for the final class) → top up via Goals and Seminars so manual sessions stay on the primary.

Bottom line / 总结: ultimate-path skill is the priority; detours are a cheap C-rank early detour you slot in on the side — and skip an off-type detour if its skill is a bane or its ability won't be used.

Q10. All FREE (0 Activity Point) actions during exploration

These cost no Activity Point (some cost gold / bait / Renown instead) — do them every Explore day before spending AP:

  • Talk to / greet students & NPCs — support points, recruit chats, occasional items.
  • Return lost items to the owner — support + rewards (gold / Renown / gifts).
  • Fishing — uses bait, not AP; professor EXP + fish for meals.
  • Gardening (Plant → Cultivate → Harvest) — uses gold to cultivate, not AP; professor EXP + stat boosters, seeds, flowers.
  • Pick up glowing items scattered around the monastery.
  • Marketplace shopping, Blacksmith (forge/repair), Battalion Guild (hire/repair) — cost gold, not AP.
  • Quests / bulletin board — accept and turn in; plus the Advice Box.
  • Saint Statue donations — cost Renown, not AP.
  • Walking / strolling itself.

For contrast, the AP-COST activities are: Dining / Share a Meal, Cooking, Choir Practice, Tea Party, Faculty Training, Tournament, Sparring (and Sauna in the DLC). Spend AP on these; everything in the list above is free.

Q11. How many Beginner and Intermediate seals do I need?

Short answer: about one Beginner Seal per unit, and one Intermediate Seal per intermediate class each unit certifies into — which includes every intermediate mastery detour (Brigand, Archer, Mage, and Pegasus Knight are all intermediate, so each detour eats an Intermediate Seal). For a ~13-unit Azure Moon roster that's roughly ~10–13 Beginner and ~25–30+ Intermediate seals across the whole run.

Key facts

  • A seal is consumed whether you PASS or FAIL the exam — only certify when you actually meet the requirement (guaranteed pass), or you waste the seal.
  • Both are buyable at the Marketplace — Beginner Seal 500g, Intermediate Seal 1,000g. Beginner seals are plentiful; Intermediate seals have limited stock per week early (they restock), so buy them whenever they're available.
  • One certification = one seal. Beginner classes (Myrmidon/Soldier/Fighter/Monk) use a Beginner Seal; intermediate classes (Mercenary, Cavalier, Brigand, Archer, Mage, Priest, Pegasus Knight, Brawler…) use an Intermediate Seal.
  • Late joiners arrive pre-classed (Catherine = Swordmaster, Gilbert = Fortress), so you skip their early seal costs.

Practical: don't ration Beginner/Intermediate seals the way you ration Advanced/Master ones — they're cheap and restock. Just buy Intermediate seals whenever the shop has them, and never attempt an exam you might fail.
