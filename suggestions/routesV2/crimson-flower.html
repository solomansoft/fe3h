Route 2 — Crimson Flower V2

Black Eagles → Empire · Hard · Byleth ♂. Run 2 of 4. Side with Edelgard against the Church in Ch.11. Short, fast route; final maps punish ground units, so lean on fliers.
Byleth gender: Male — CF is the one route where male Byleth is fully supported (Byleth × Edelgard works for male, and male unlocks War Master).
🎂 Birthday — set early Harpstring Moon (Month 5), on an exploration week. Edelgard mails the Black Eagle Pendant (Charm +2), collected in both Parts (two copies) if the date lands on a monastery week (avoid mission/locked weeks). Charm matters beyond flavour — it decides the White Heron Cup (your Dancer, Petra), helps Charm-gated recruits, and boosts gambit Hit/damage — equip the pendants on Petra (Dancer) and Byleth. Bonus: other units' birthdays give a free, auto-accepted tea party (no C-support gate) for cheap support.
Roster size: 13 with DLC (Anna 🧩) / 12 non-DLC. ⚠️ Crimson Flower is the game's smallest roster and cannot reach 15: only the 8 Black Eagles + Byleth + Jeritza + 2 faculty (Manuela, Hanneman) are recruitable — the Knights of Seiros (Catherine, Alois) and other Church units are enemies on the anti-Church route. So you deploy ≤12 with only 0–1 adjutant bodies; lean on Battalions to compensate. (Anna, DLC, is the only path to a 13th.)
V2 plan: rebuilt around the path/detour principle — route each unit's class path through mastery classes on their boon/neutral weapons and harvest those masteries; skip masteries gated behind a bane weapon. Hard difficulty → run fuller skill sets, not the Normal lean-kit (R12).

V2 build basis

This plan is built from each unit's boon/bane profile (the boon makes a weapon cheap to rank; the bane makes it costly — so we avoid detouring through bane-gated mastery classes). Community consensus cross-checked against Game8, GamerSky, GameWith, Fandom, GameFAQs, and Reddit (r/FireEmblemThreeHouses).

Cert ranks for the mastery-detour classes used below:

  • Brigand → Axe C (yields Death Blow)
  • Archer → Bow C (yields Hit+20)
  • Mage → Reason C (yields Fiendish Blow)
  • Pegasus Knight → Lance C + Flying D — FEMALE-ONLY (yields Darting Blow)

Key V2 corrections vs V1:

  • Edelgard: Bow is her bane → drop Archer/Hit+20. Use Defiant Crit / Armored Effect− (Vengeance+Vantage optional). Axe is a boon → Death Blow stays.
  • Jeritza: Pegasus Knight is female-only and Jeritza is male → the V1 "Pegasus → Darting Blow" line is impossible. Drop it.
  • Bernadetta: Axe is her bane → no Brigand/Death Blow (community: "poor investment"). Bow boon → Hit+20 only.
  • Manuela: Reason is her bane → drop Mage/Fiendish Blow; lean white-magic + Faith skills.

🔒 Route-exclusive highlights (verified)

  • Edelgard — playable only on Crimson Flower.
  • Hubert — playable only here; leaves if you side with the Church.
  • Jeritza — free, NOT DLC (ships with the free 1.1.0 update). Auto-joins start of Part 2 (~Ch.13) as a Lv27 Death Knight, no recruitment.
  • Church units are NOT available here (Seteth, Flayn, Catherine, Cyril, Shamir).

Roster composition — built as a team (R9)

  • Damage split: Well balanced. Magic: Hubert (Dark Knight), Dorothea (Gremory), Hanneman (Warlock, flex), Manuela (Holy Knight white-magic, flex). Physical: Byleth (War Master), Edelgard (Emperor), Jeritza (Wyvern Lord), Ferdinand (Paladin), Caspar (War Master), Bernadetta (bow).
  • Healing/staff: Linhardt (Bishop, Warp/Physic primary) + Dorothea (Faith → Physic, Gremory doubles uses) + Manuela (mounted Holy Knight healer, flex). Solid.
  • Fliers / mobility: Jeritza + Anna (Wyvern Lord) and Edelgard (armored, no flight) for offense; Ferdinand (Paladin) + Hubert/Manuela (mounted) give Canto. Linhardt's Warp repositions the slow brawlers (Byleth/Caspar).
  • Ranged / effective coverage: Bernadetta (Sniper/Bow Knight, anti-flier) + Hubert/Dorothea/Hanneman magic (anti-armor) + 1–2 range from mages. CF's late maps reward fliers + bows — both present.
  • Gambits/Authority: Edelgard + Hubert carry the strongest battalions (Authority boons); Byleth/Caspar want a Stride/Warp escort for low Move.
  • Composition picks flagged below: Ferdinand → Paladin (#1 anyway; reuse-variant vs SS Wyvern Lord); Bernadetta runs Sniper→Bow Knight for mobility; Manuela → Holy Knight as a composition pick (mobile healer that avoids redundancy with Linhardt's Bishop — community default for her is Bishop; noted); Hanneman flex deepens CF's thin caster bench.

Recruitment plan & timing (R11)

Recruit selection (R11.1) — identical procedure on every route: forced free core (house + 🔒 + Byleth) → compatible-gated recruits → too-good-to-miss exceptions (support-waiver) → fill to 15 from the free Lv15-only pool by role → combat-ready → lowest reuse → alphabetical → exclude off-build non-essentials. Target size 15 (Crimson Flower capped at 12–13).

🆓 Free recruits (Empire route — the shallowest pool). Your entire Black Eagles house auto-joins for free (no gate) — that's your depth. The only actual recruits are Manuela and Hanneman (modest gates — grab both) plus Anna 🧩 (DLC, Ch.3). ⚠️ The Knights of Seiros (Catherine, Alois) and other Church units cannot be recruited on the anti-Church route — which is exactly why CF caps at 12–13 and can't reach a 15-body roster.

  • Anna 🧩 (DLC) — appears Ch.3, recruit freely (no stat reqs). Skip if no Expansion Pass.
  • Jeritza (free, NOT DLC) — auto-joins start of Part 2 (~Ch.13) as a Lv27 Death Knight. Reclass out of Death Knight immediately and build Flying.
  • Manuela, Hanneman (faculty recruits) — recruit before the Ch.11–12 point of no return; meet their (modest Charm/Authority) stat gates early so they aren't under-leveled. R11: don't grind an off-build skill on Byleth just to recruit — use support/passive stats or skip.
  • All other units are native Black Eagles. Push Ferdinand onto Paladin, Jeritza/Anna onto Wyvern Lord, and Byleth/Caspar onto War Master.

Romance / pairings this route — best-by-default, with allowed easy-route diversity (R5)

Crimson Flower is the second of the two easy routes (Hard), so per R5 a diversity swap is permitted here too. Most units take their best pairing. (Pairings carry over unchanged from V1 — the boon/bane re-routing of classes does not affect supports.)

  • Byleth ♂ S-support → Edelgard 🔒 (CF-exclusive; works for male Byleth).

Non-Byleth paired endings (4):

  • Ferdinand × Dorothea — best-by-default (mutual confirmed pairing; Songstress/Confidence synergy). ✔ best
  • Caspar × Linhardt — best-by-default (mutual confirmed best support; fixes Caspar's Move). ✔ best
  • Hanneman × Manuela — best-by-default for both (the classic mutual-best "bickering" pair). Intentional best-pairing REPEAT (R5.2 allows it). ✔ best (repeat)
  • Petra × Bernadetta — ⚠️ DIVERSITY pick — deliberately sub-optimal. Why it's a weak choice: Petra's strongest partner is Petra × Dorothea, but Dorothea is locked to her mutual-best Ferdinand here, so Petra is pulled onto a lesser chain; Bernadetta's best is Byleth (taken by Edelgard) with only thin alternatives. It's a confirmed support but not either unit's optimal — used only to add variety and pad the count, acceptable solely because Crimson Flower is one of the easy routes.
  • Genuinely unpairable on this roster: Hubert (canon-devoted to Edelgard, who is Byleth-locked here), Jeritza (only S-tie is his sister Mercedes — not romantic, not on CF), and Anna (cannot support anyone). All combat-only.

What a "diversity pairing" is, and why it's a bad choice (R5): it trades a unit's best support — and that support's affinity/stat bonus and combat synergy — for a weaker but different one, just to showcase variety across runs. It's a deliberate cost, allowed only on the easy routes (never Maddening). Here that swap is Petra × Bernadetta (Petra's #1 partner Dorothea is taken by Ferdinand).
Best-pairing repeat: Hanneman × Manuela — both are each other's best; R5 allows repeating a best pairing.
