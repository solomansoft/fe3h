Catherine / 卡多莉奴 — Gameplay Recommendations

Fire Emblem: Three Houses — Knights of Seiros (recruit from ~Ch.4 on BL/GD; auto on Silver Snow; enemy on Crimson Flower) Sources: EN (fe3h.com, Game8, GameFAQs), JP (GameWith, note.com/Altema), CN (GamerSky).

Snapshot

Joins as a Level 11 Swordmaster with Sword A, monster offensive stats (high Str/Spd/Dex), the Major Crest of Charon, and the Hero's Relic Thunderbrand. One of the best early-recruitable combat units in the game — recruit her ASAP. [GamerSky CN: "女剑圣有唯一真神器雷霆" / female Swordmaster with the unique relic Thunderbrand.]

Class-change path (stack-ranked, to Master tier)

  1. #1 — Swordmaster (剑圣) — stay [Game8, GameWith JP, fe3h.com, GamerSky CN]. - Cert: Sword A (Advanced Seal). She already meets it at recruitment — zero tutoring needed. - Rationale: maximizes Thunderbrand + Foudroyant Strike (her Crest-gated combat art), +4 Spd class bonus for guaranteed doubles, and synergizes with her personal Fighting Spirit (-5 damage when battalion has 0 endurance) — i.e. she's "unnaturally tanky" running no/low battalion. [Game8] Push Sword to S+ for Swordfaire.
  2. #2 — Wyvern Lord (飞龙领主) [GameFAQs/Game8 EN alt; Maddening crowd]. - Cert: Lance C / Axe A / Flying A — requires training Axe + Flying (she has neither natively). ⚠️ Needs significant tutoring. - Rationale: on Maddening the Swordmaster's 5 move and sword's lower Might fall off; Wyvern Lord gives 8 move, flight, Canto and axe Might. Pick this only if you're not committed to Thunderbrand. [GameFAQs EN: "Wyvern Lord is a good alternative if you don't plan to use her relic."]
  3. #3 — Assassin / Hero — Assassin for mobility+stealth keeping swords; Hero for Vantage/axes. Niche; weaker than the top two.

⚠️ Tutoring summary: Swordmaster = none. Wyvern Lord = tutor Axe and Flying heavily.

Mastery detours / skills to grab

Classes to certify into ONLY to fill the class-mastery bar and learn the permanent mastery ability, then leave — these are NOT part of the final class path above. - Brigand → Death Blow (+6 Str when this unit initiates combat) — top priority; extra player-phase damage stacking on Thunderbrand. [GameFAQs EN] - Archer → Hit +20 (+20 Hit) — fixes Swordmaster accuracy, called out explicitly for her. [GameFAQs EN] - Mercenary → Vantage (attack first when foe initiates and HP < 50%) — pairs with her crit + relic for a deadly enemy-phase Vantage/Wrath setup. - Myrmidon → Speed +2 — cheap early permanent Spd bump if you take her through the sword beginner line.

Training focus

  • Sword → S+ for Swordfaire (big damage boost). [GameFAQs EN]
  • Authority → C for Battalion Vantage; her relic + crit make Vantage/Wrath enemy-phase deadly. [fe3h.com]
  • Bow dip → temporary Archer for Hit +20 to fix her accuracy. [GameFAQs EN]
  • Axe dip → Brigand for Death Blow if you want extra damage. [GameFAQs EN]
  • Combat arts learned natively: Bane of Monsters (Sword C+), Finesse Blade (Sword A); plus Foudroyant Strike via Thunderbrand + Major Crest of Charon.

Supports / pairings

  • Byleth (S-support, strong story + stat path), Shamir (their paired support is a fan favorite), Seteth, Dimitri, Ashe, Gilbert.
  • Pair-up adjutant: a defensive adjutant or speed adjutant to keep her doubling.

Weapon / item / battalion / skill loadout

  • Weapon: Thunderbrand (relic) as her signature; Killing Edge / Brave Sword / Silver Sword+ as backups; can use Thunder (Reason) as ranged chip.
  • Combat arts: Foudroyant Strike, Finesse Blade, Bane of Monsters.
  • 5 equipped skills (suggested): Swordfaire, Death Blow, Hit +20, Battalion Vantage (or Vantage), Sword Crit +10 / Darting Blow.
  • Battalion/Gambit: if running her tanky personal skill, leave battalion empty or use a high-crit-avoid battalion; otherwise a crit/offense battalion + Retribution for enemy-phase.

Ranked options & disagreements

  1. Swordmaster (stay) — [Game8 EN], [GameWith JP], [fe3h.com EN], [GamerSky CN] consensus; best for Thunderbrand.
  2. Wyvern Lord — [GameFAQs EN Maddening] for those skipping the relic; better move/Might at high difficulty.
  3. GameWith JP nuance: keep Swordmaster but master Mercenary/Brigand abilities first (Death Blow etc.) before settling. [GameWith JP] - Disagreement: EN Maddening players say Swordmaster's 5 move + sword Might "becomes really undesirable" lategame; casual/relic players keep Swordmaster. Tier placements vary — [note.com JP] only C-tier (penalizes her as a non-magic female who can't reach the strongest niches), but most EN sources rate her a top early combat unit. - Verdict on investment: Strongly worth it — she arrives combat-ready, needs no setup, and Thunderbrand is one of the best relics.
