Recommendation — Ferdinand von Aegir / 菲尔迪南特·冯·埃吉尔

Fire Emblem: Three Houses — Black Eagles 黑鹫学级 Gameplay build recommendations (English). Grounded in objective data: balanced growths (HP 50% / Str 45% / Spd 50% / Cha 40%), no weaknesses, strengths Sword·Lance·Axe·Riding, budding talent Heavy Armor (unlocks Seal Speed), personal Confidence (Hit/Avo +15 at full HP), Minor Crest of Cichol.

Class-change path

  1. #1 Noble → Soldier → Cavalier → Paladin — his signature build. Lance B + Riding B is easy (both strengths). Paladin + Death Blow (from Brigand) + Lancefaire makes his Swift Strikes combat art do ~+22 damage — one of the best player-phase tools in the game. Move 8 + Canto. [GameFAQs] [GamerSky CN] [Game8]
  2. #2 Noble → Fighter → Brigand → Wyvern Rider → Wyvern Lord — best raw min-max. Trades Lancefaire for Move 8 flying + better growths and Defiant Crit. Pairs with Alert Stance+ for a premier dodge-tank (his Confidence gives Hit/Avo +15 at full HP). ⚠️ Needs Flying A (not a strength — tutor it; Axe is already a strength). [GameFAQs] [note.com JP]
  3. #3 Noble → ... → Great Knight — uses his Heavy Armor budding talent, but sources agree it's "inadvisable to over-invest"; Great Knight's high skill demand gives marginal gain over Paladin. [GameFAQs] [Pegasusknight JP]
  4. Alternative: Hero (Sword B + Axe C, both strengths) for Defiant Str + versatility on a sword-axe build. [Pegasusknight JP]

No weakness blocks any of these — he is one of the most flexible units in the game.

Mastery detours / skills to grab

Classes to certify into ONLY to fill the class-mastery bar and learn the permanent mastery ability, then leave — these are NOT part of his final class path (Cavalier → Paladin, or Wyvern Lord). 1. Brigand → Death Blow (Str +6 when he initiates — pushes his Swift Strikes to ~+22 damage; surfaced from his loadout's "Death Blow" entry). 2. Pegasus Knight → Darting Blow (Spd +5 when he initiates — surfaced from his "Darting Blow / Alert Stance+" loadout note; great for securing doubles on his player-phase build. ⚠️ Pegasus Knight is female-only, so this detour is unavailable to him — prefer raising Speed via boosters/Cavalier instead). 3. Cavalier → Desperation (if he initiates at HP < 50%, his follow-up lands before the enemy counter — a free pickup since Cavalier is on his Paladin path).

Training focus

  • Core for #1: Lance to A (Lancefaire + Swift Strikes), Riding to B+.
  • For Wyvern/dodge build: Axe to A, Flying to A, Authority for Alert Stance+ battalion access.
  • Budding talent: Heavy Armor → Seal Speed — situationally nice (debuffs a fast foe) but don't chase Heavy Armor for the class; just unlock the ability if convenient.
  • Keep his HP topped up to keep Confidence active (avoid chip-damage tiles).

Supports / pairings

  • Edelgard / Hubert — strong story + combat supports; he frontlines alongside the lord.
  • Byleth (S-support) — well-regarded romance; good adjacency bonuses.
  • Dorothea — popular pairing; her Songstress heals him to keep Confidence up. Strong synergy.
  • Petra — cavalry/flier speed pair covering each other.

Weapon / item / battalion / skill loadout

  • Weapons: Lance — Swift Strikes is the centerpiece; equip Silver Lance+ / Killer Lance; relic Lance of Ruin if available. Axe (Wyvern build): Killer Axe / Brave Axe.
  • Combat arts: Swift Strikes (Lance, his bread-and-butter), Tempest Lance, Sunder.
  • Equipped skills (5): Lancefaire (Paladin) · Death Blow · Darting Blow / Alert Stance+ · Hit +20 or Defiant Crit · Confidence-supporting skill (e.g., a +Avo skill for dodge builds).
  • Battalion/gambit: a high-Avo or high-Charm battalion (he has decent Charm); Retribution for enemy-phase, or Stride. Alert Stance+ battalion for dodge build.
  • Key items: Speed Ring + Strength booster; a Goddess Ring or Avo accessory for the dodge variant.

Ranked options & disagreements

  1. Paladin — #1 well-rounded, Swift Strikes + Lancefaire + Death Blow. [GameFAQs, GamerSky, Game8]
  2. Wyvern Lord — #1 for min-max/dodge-tank, if you tutor Flying. [GameFAQs, note.com]
  3. Hero — solid sword/axe alt. [Pegasusknight]
  4. Great Knight — last; marginal over Paladin despite his budding talent. [Pegasusknight, GameFAQs] - Disagreement: Game8 lists Paladin and Great Knight together; GameFAQs/Pegasusknight push Paladin over Great Knight and float Wyvern as the true ceiling. Consensus: Paladin default, Wyvern for ceiling.
