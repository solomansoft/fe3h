Recommendation — Hubert von Vestra / 修伯特·冯·贝斯特拉

Fire Emblem: Three Houses — Black Eagles 黑鹫学级 Gameplay build recommendations (English). Grounded in objective data: Mag 55% growth (highest in Black Eagles), Spd 45%, no Crest, strengths Bow·Reason·Authority, weaknesses Axe·Faith·Flying, budding talent Lance (unlocks Frozen Lance). Locked to the Black Eagles route (cannot be recruited).

Class-change path

  1. #1 Noble → Monk → Mage → Dark Bishop → Dark Knight — the consensus best. Dark Knight is the only class with Dark Tomefaire (his spell list is mostly dark magic: Mire, Banshee, etc.), plus Move 7 mobility and Lance access for Frozen Lance. Grab Fiendish Blow from Mage and consider Lifetaker from Dark Bishop. ⚠️ Needs Riding A for Dark Knight (not a strength — tutor it) and Lance C. [Game8] [GameWith JP] [Pegasusknight JP]
  2. #2 Noble → Monk → Mage → Warlock → Dark Bishop (stay) — pure damage caster if you don't want to grind Riding. Note: Warlock's Black Tomefaire does NOT boost his dark spells, so it's a downgrade vs Dark Knight for him; Dark Bishop gives extra dark-spell uses. [GameFAQs]
  3. #3 Noble → Monk → Mage → Bishop/Gremory — only if you build him toward Faith/white magic, which fights his Faith weakness; not recommended. ⚠️ Faith weakness must be tutored to certify Bishop/Gremory.

Important: GamerSky CN explicitly warns "do NOT build him as a black-magic (Reason) Warlock — his kit is dark magic." This aligns with the Dark Knight pick.

Mastery detours / skills to grab

Classes to certify into ONLY to fill the class-mastery bar and learn the permanent mastery ability, then leave — these are NOT part of his final class path (Mage → Dark Bishop → Dark Knight). 1. Mage → Fiendish Blow (Mag +6 when he initiates — the universal caster detour; surfaced from his loadout's "Fiendish Blow." He certifies through Mage anyway). 2. Dark Mage → Poison Strike (fixed post-combat chip damage — surfaced from his loadout's "Poison Strike (Dark Mage mastery)"; a clean offensive pickup on the dark-magic track). 3. Dark Bishop → Lifetaker (recover HP after he kills in combat — surfaced from his "consider Lifetaker from Dark Bishop"; Dark Bishop is an intermediate on his path, so he can master it before moving to Dark Knight). 4. Monk → Magic +2 (flat +2 Mag — cheap early grab while certifying through Monk).

Training focus

  • Core: Reason to A (his spells), Riding to A (Dark Knight — tutor, it's his bottleneck), Lance to C (Frozen Lance + Dark Knight req).
  • Budding talent: Lance → Frozen Lance — a magic-scaling lance combat art; very strong early on Maddening and gives him a melee option. Worth unlocking.
  • Authority (strength) for gambits — his personal Officer Duty gives gambit Might +5.
  • Don't bother with Faith or Axe.

Supports / pairings

  • Edelgard (auto, story) — his defining partner; he provides ranged magic + gambit support behind her frontline.
  • Ferdinand — good combat support (and great banter); cavalry partner covering his flanks.
  • Romance is limited for M!Hubert (Edelgard is story-locked to Byleth on CF), but supports with Edelgard/Ferdinand/Bernadetta give useful adjacency bonuses.
  • Synergy: his Officer Duty + high Mag make him an excellent gambit-debuffer to set up allies.

Weapon / item / battalion / skill loadout

  • Weapons/spells: Reason — Mire (his signature long-range dark spell), Banshee, Death Γ; equip a Bolt Axe? no (axe weakness) — stick to tomes; carry Frozen Lance via a Lance.
  • Combat arts: Frozen Lance (Lance).
  • Equipped skills (5): Dark Tomefaire (Dark Knight) · Fiendish Blow · Lifetaker · Poison Strike (Dark Mage mastery) · Authority skill / Battalion Vantage. (Swap in Bowbreaker situationally.)
  • Battalion/gambit: a Mag/Authority battalion; his Officer Duty makes any gambit a strong debuff/finisher. Retribution works on a frontline-adjacent build.
  • Key items: Spirit Dust (Magic boosters) prioritized; a Speed Ring helps secure doubles.

Ranked options & disagreements

  1. Dark Knight — #1, only Dark Tomefaire class + mobility. [Game8, GameWith, Pegasusknight]
  2. Dark Bishop (terminal) — #2, extra dark-spell uses if you skip the Riding grind. [GameFAQs]
  3. Warlock — #3, redundant Black Tomefaire; explicitly cautioned against. [GamerSky CN, GameFAQs]
  4. Bishop/Gremory — lowest; fights his Faith weakness. - Disagreement: GamerSky CN once flagged the Dark Knight Riding grind as "questionable" and floated Cavalier/Dark Mage intermediates — resolved here as: Dark Mage→Dark Knight is the clean path; the Riding tutoring is the only friction.
