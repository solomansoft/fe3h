Monastery Daily Activities — General Mechanics Reference (route-agnostic)

Scope. This is the general, route-agnostic reference for the Garreg Mach monastery daily/weekly systems: what each activity does, when to pick it, and how to spend Activity Points well. It is built from the GamerSky (游民星空) Simplified-Chinese walkthrough (translated to English) cross-checked against this project's existing notes.

It deliberately does NOT repeat the route-specific plans. For a concrete week-by-week plan use: - blue-lions-azure-moon-guide.md — the Azure Moon (Blue Lions) walkthrough and training schedule: calendar, mission/paralogue deadlines, Professor-Level table, the Explore/Battle/Seminar/Rest budget, and the day-by-day who-to-tutor / weekend-activity plan for Run 1.

Those stay route-specific. This file is the mechanics layer they sit on top of.

Facts policy. Confirmed mechanics only. GamerSky-sourced numbers are tagged where they originate; anything not fully confirmed is marked ⚠️/verify. Sources cached in /sources/gamersky/monastery-daily-activities.md.

1. The weekly day-type choice: Explore / Battle / Seminar / Rest

Each free day (typically the weekend; the rest of the week is mostly fixed Instruction/story days) you pick exactly one:

Day-type What it does Pick it when…
Exploration (散步) Roam the monastery and spend Activity Points on meals, choir, tea, fishing, gardening, faculty training, the tournament, sparring, shop/forge, returning lost items, quests, talking to people. Highest support / Professor-EXP / motivation / item yield. Default. Especially the first free day of a new month — the monastery refreshes with new quests, glowing items, and dialogue.
Battle (出击) Fight an auxiliary skirmish and/or an available Paralogue. Gives XP, gold, skill XP, items. Gives no motivation. A paralogue is open, units are underleveled, or you need gold/skill XP. Often best on the last free day of the month after instruction is done.
Seminar (讲习/研讨) Byleth + several students whose goals match the focus gain a big block of skill XP and motivation, with little micromanagement. Rushing several units toward a certification / master class without spending an explore day.
Rest (修养) Restores Motivation broadly to your students (and repairs the Sword of the Creator). No meals/items. Motivation is dead across the board and you have nothing urgent. Lowest priority.

Default priority: Explore > Battle > Seminar > Rest.

2. Activity Points (行动点数)

  • You get a fixed number of points per Exploration day, scaling with Professor Level (see §3). They are spent only on the "hourglass-icon" activities (meals, choir, tea, tournament, sparring, some quests).
  • Free actions (no points): walking, talking, picking up glowing items, returning lost items, fishing, gardening, shopping/forging, the Advice Box.
  • Running out of points doesn't end the stroll — you just can't do more point-cost activities. Spend every point each Explore day; never end a day with points unused.

3. Professor / Instruction Level (指导等级) — the master multiplier

Raising Professor Level is the single highest-leverage thing you do, because it controls: - Activity Points per Explore day, - the number of Adjutants (副官) you can assign, and - Shop inventory (better goods unlock at higher levels).

Ranks run E → A+. Per GamerSky, A+ requires 44,500 total EXP. (For the exact per-level AP/Battle-Pt/Instruct-Pt/Funds/Adjutant table this project uses, see day-by-day §0.2 — not repeated here.)

What raises Professor Level fastest (GamerSky EXP figures)

Activity Professor EXP Cost Notes
Combat Tournament (武斗大会) 300 win / 100 loss-or-withdraw 1 AP Best EXP-per-point; needs no motivation.
Lecture monthly question (授课提问) 300–1200 by answer quality free (1×/month) Also boosts the asking student's motivation (see §5).
Dining / shared meal (用餐) up to 200 1 AP Also support + motivation.
Choir practice (合唱) 50–200 1 AP Also support + Faith EXP (participants) + Byleth Authority EXP. No motivation.
Cooking (烹饪) 25–100 1 AP Chance of a temporary day-of-battle stat buff.
Fishing (钓鱼) 10–30 per catch, scales with fish star rating 0 AP Effectively unlimited while you have bait — free passive EXP.
Gardening (园艺) 100–500 on harvest, scales with seed quality 0 AP Free passive EXP every visit.

Fast-leveling rule of thumb: answer the monthly question well, do the tournament when AP is scarce, and treat fishing + gardening as free EXP you collect on every single Explore day.

4. Instruction / tutoring (讲习)

  • Spend Instruct Points to drill individual students' weapon/magic skills. A Great/Perfect result lets you Praise (refunds motivation), so chase those on priority units.
  • Motivation (干劲): each tutoring session costs the student motivation; higher motivation = more sessions allowed. Refill via meals, tea (only after it unlocks — see §10), gifts, good lecture answers, the sauna (DLC), and Rest days. ⚠️ Choir does NOT restore motivation — it gives Faith EXP + support + Byleth Authority EXP only (see §9).
  • Group Tasks (团队任务): weekly co-op assignment pairing two students → resources + skill XP + mutual support between the pair. Always accept them (free support/XP).
  • Goals / self-study: set each student's goal at their target-class skills; they self-study toward it. Change goals as plans change.
  • Adjutants (副官): unlocked by Professor Level; a back-row unit attached to a front-line unit for extra attacks/support and steady support growth.

5. Lecture question system (授课提问)

The first class of each month, one student asks a 3-option question. - Best answer: +50 Motivation (~2 bars) and up to 1200 Professor EXP. - Neutral: +25 Motivation (1 bar), up to 1000 EXP. - Poor: +0 Motivation, up to ~1000 EXP (occasionally still nudges motivation).

Per-character correct answers differ between Part 1 and Part 2. (Answer tables are out of scope here — see the per-character question guides.)

6. Faculty Training (教官训练) — raising Byleth's own skills

  • A point-cost Explore activity (unlocks around Ch.3 ⚠️ verify per route): Byleth trains a chosen skill, including off-type skills (e.g. pushing Reason for a Mortal Savant build). This is the main way to grow Byleth's ranks, since Byleth doesn't take lectures. The route schedules lean on this for off-type rank gates.

7. Seminars (讲习/研讨)

  • A weekend day-type (§1) where Byleth + several matching students gain a large block of skill XP. Use it to rush multiple units toward a certification at once.

8. Meals / shared dining (食堂)

  • Invite 2 characters → raises support among diners + Motivation; serving a liked dish gives a bigger boost. 1 AP; also gives Professor EXP.
  • Best practice: invite two characters who already share a support chain to push it forward, and pick a dish both like.

9. Choir practice (大教堂)

  • Pick 2 participants → support for both, Faith EXP (participants), Authority EXP for Byleth, and Professor EXP. 1 AP. Great for faith-track units and the main way to passively push Byleth's Authority. ⚠️ Choir does NOT restore motivation — common misconception; some CN guides (incl. this project's cached GamerSky note) wrongly list it. Verified against Game8 / GameWith EN: motivation comes from meals/tea/gifts/rest/lecture, not choir.

10. Tea Party (茶会) — mechanics

  • Availability ⚠️ (NOT from the start): Tea parties unlock by completing Ferdinand's "Tea for Two" (双人茶会) quest, first offered in Chapter 4 (Blue Sea Moon, ~July). Complete it to turn tea on early; if you skip it, the feature auto-unlocks only at Chapter 13 (post-timeskip). There are no tea parties before Ch.4 — early-game support must come from meals, gifts, lost items, and group tasks instead. [Fandom; GameWith]
  • 1 AP; free and always-accepted on the guest's birthday (non-birthday guests may decline). Eligible: own-house students, or other-house students at C-support+.
  • Pick a tea first. Any tea works, but a favored tea smooths it. Universal teas (liked by everyone): Hresvelg Blend and Leicester Cortania.
  • 3 topic rounds (15s, 3 options each; ≥1 is liked). Correct → support up; wrong → no penalty.
  • Standard tea: need 3 correct to unlock the final round. Favored tea: only 2 needed.
  • Final round → Free Time: observe the guest (look at head/body for a reaction) or give a gift. Completing it = big support gain +1 Charm to both participants. (For per-character topics see ../../conversation.md §4.)

11. Fishing (钓鱼)

  • Needs bait; 0 AP, unlimited casts. Yields gold, food ingredients, and Professor EXP scaling with fish star rating. Event/"magic-fluctuation" days spawn much better fish/gold — fish hard on those days. The fishing-pond Saint-statue upgrade improves catches.

12. Gardening / cultivation (园艺)

  • Plant seeds in the greenhouse; 0 AP, harvest gives Professor EXP (100–500 by seed quality). Cultivation produces stat-booster items, flowers (gifts), and rare seeds. Better seeds + good combos → better yields (including stat boosters). Harvest every visit.

13. Returning Lost Items (失物)

  • Glowing items found while exploring; 0 AP. Return each to its correct owner → support up. Each character has 3 lost items (Byleth has 1 tutorial item; one character has none). Wrong owner = no effect. (Owner table: GamerSky lost-items guide, cached.)

14. Gifts (送礼)

  • Give a liked gift (or a special gift like feathers) → larger support/affection than a generic one. For non-house students this raises recruitment favor; for your own it raises support. Sources: glowing explore items, 2 marketplace merchants, bulletin-board rewards, and gardening flowers.

15. Advice Box / counseling (信件 / 建议箱)

  • Read students' letters/worries with the advisor and pick the helpful reply → support up and resolves the concern. Free.

16. Quests / Requests (任务 / 布告栏)

  • Bulletin boards in each monastery area list weekly quests/announcements and fast-travel to quest NPCs. Rewards: gold, Renown, gifts, items, recipes. Clear them every Explore day. (Completing the three starter tasks in Month 4 grants a full +1 Professor Level.)

17. Sauna (DLC — Expansion Pass)

  • ⚠️ Not documented in the GamerSky pages crawled for this file. The Abyss sauna (Cindered Shadows / Expansion Pass) lets you bathe with a guest for a support boost and a temporary stat/Charm-style buff. Treat specifics as verify against a DLC-specific source.

18. Renown / Saint Statues (名声 / 圣人像)

  • Renown is earned from battles, quests, and exploration. Spend it at the cathedral's Saint Statues to unlock permanent buffs (more EXP/support/gold, better fishing/gardening, extra adjutant slots, etc.) and to recover lost units/stats.
  • Priority: invest early — the EXP/support/gold multipliers compound over the whole run.

19. Shop / Forge / Smithing (商店 / 锻造)

  • Marketplace merchants' stock grows with Professor Level. The Smithy/Forge upgrades weapons and repairs durability using Smithing Stones (锻造石). Saint-statue upgrades improve shop/forge offerings — another reason to bank Renown early.

20. Stat-booster priorities (general)

  • Stat boosters come from gardening combos, certain quests/bulletin rewards, and chests. Pour them into your carry units and into stats their class can't grow well (e.g. boosters that fix a unit's worst stat for its intended class). ⚠️ Exact best-recipe/best-target lists are route- and roster-specific — see the route activity schedule rather than this general file.

21. Daily / weekly optimal routine (priority order)

A general loop for any house (the route files specialize it):

Each Exploration day: 1. Pick up everything (glowing items) and return all lost items you can match (free support). 2. Fish through your bait and harvest/replant the greenhouse (free Professor EXP + ingredients + stat boosters/gifts). 3. Clear bulletin-board quests (gold, Renown, items). 4. Spend AP, highest value first: Tournament (Professor EXP) → Meal with a support pair (support + motivation) → Choir with a faith / Byleth-Authority pair (no motivation) → Tea Party with a priority/birthday character (only once tea unlocks in Ch.4). 5. Read the Advice Box and give a liked gift to a recruit target / S-support target (free). 6. Bank Renown → Saint Statue upgrades early; visit Forge to repair/upgrade key weapons.

Each week / month: - Answer the monthly lecture question well (big motivation + Professor EXP). - Spend Instruct Points on priority units; accept every Group Task. - Use Faculty Training to push Byleth's off-type skills toward a class gate. - Switch the weekend to Battle when a paralogue is open or units are underleveled; Seminar to rush a certification; Rest only when motivation is broadly dead.

For the concrete who/when version of this loop on Azure Moon, follow blue-lions-azure-moon-guide.md.
