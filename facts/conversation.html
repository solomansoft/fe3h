Fire Emblem: Three Houses — Optimal Conversation Choices Guide (Full Game)

Switch • English. Covers White Clouds (Part 1, Ch.1–12) and all four Part 2 routes (Azure Moon, Crimson Flower, Silver Snow, Verdant Wind), plus Tea Parties, Goddess Tower, dining/choir, flag choices, and the Cindered Shadows DLC.

When more than one answer gives the same bonus, all correct answers are listed (separated by "OR").

Sources: RPG Site, SAMURAI GAMERS, Shacknews, Serenes Forest, Game8, Fire Emblem Wiki/Fandom, and GamerSky (游民星空, Simplified Chinese — translated) for the per-chapter affinity choices. 1. How dialogue choices matter

Type of choice Effect
Cutscene ("In-Game") prompts Some give support points with the speaker (a student, faculty, or your house leader). Most others are pure flavor.
Explore conversations (talking to wandering students/NPCs) The "right" reply gives support points with that character. The wrong reply almost always = no bonus, no penalty.
Advice / "what should you do" prompts during a unit's turn Don't grant support; they reveal canon answers/lore (flavor).
Lecture / question-box (during instruction) Correct answers raise that student's Motivation and can raise your Professor Level progress (not story support).
Route-decision / flag choices Permanently change the route, recruitment, or who lives/dies (Section 7).

Key facts: - Most "wrong" choices do NOT punish you — you simply miss the bonus. - A handful of choices LOWER support — those are flagged below with ⛔ AVOID. - Initial house pick gives affinity toward that leader but does not lock recruitment. - In Part 2, RPG Site notes the post-battle/in-game leader lines generally don't affect affinity on AM/VW/SS; the Explore conversations are what grant support. (Crimson Flower still has support-bearing cutscene lines — listed.)

Other ways to raise support (no dialogue choice): fighting adjacent to a unit / sharing an adjutant, shared meals, choir practice, gifts/lost items, group tasks (Section 5).

4. Tea Party — topics that fill the mood gauge

Mechanics: Pick a tea, then 3 topic rounds (15s each). A topic the character likes = mood gauge rises ("drawn into the conversation" → "got lively" → "engaging conversation"). 3 successes unlock a final comment with a reaction option ("extra time"). Using the character's favorite tea means you only need 2 correct topics and a failed final reaction is forgiven. Reward: support points + possible +1 Charm to Byleth and/or the guest.

Universal safe teas (everyone likes): Hresvelg Blend, Leicester Cortania.

General rule: match topics to the character's LIKES, avoid DISLIKES. Combat-minded characters (Dimitri, Felix, Catherine, Dedue) → equipment/training/battalion talk; social characters (Sylvain, Dorothea, Hilda) → crushes/romance/sweets.

5. Dining, shared meals & Choir (mostly auto)

  • Shared meals: Invite 2 characters. Raises support with both diners (and a little among them) and raises their Motivation. Serving a dish a character likes gives a bigger boost (a happy "This is delicious!" reaction); a disliked dish gives less. No dialogue choice — the only "choice" is which dish to cook and whom to invite. Eat with two characters who already have a support chain to push it forward.
  • Choir practice: Pick 2 participants → small support boost for both + Faith EXP (and Authority EXP for Byleth). ⚠️ No motivation (verified vs. Game8 / GameWith EN — choir does not restore motivation, despite some CN guides). No dialogue choice; just pick partners.
  • Faculty Training, Group Tasks (stable/greenhouse/etc.): pairing two characters gives them mutual support; no dialogue.
  • Net tip: meals + choir + tea each cost 1 activity point and each nudge support — rotate them across the units you want to S-rank.

6. Goddess Tower (Ethereal Moon, Ch.7/8 area — the Ball)

Setup: During the Ethereal Moon chapter (the Ball), talk to the Gatekeeper beforehand to hear the legend. On the night of the Ball you go to the Goddess Tower and one eligible character joins you for a special scene.

Who appears: A character from your own house/army with whom you have C-support and enough points to start a B-support (you needn't have watched the B yet) and who can eventually reach S-rank. Because the partner must be in your class, mutually exclusive across houses (e.g., you can't get Hilda and Edelgard in one playthrough).

Effect of the choice: The Goddess Tower scene is a strong support-point boost and is great relationship-building — but per Game8/SAMURAI GAMERS it does NOT lock your eventual S-support/romance partner.

⚠️ The Goddess Tower scenes use a short branching dialogue per character; community sources do not consistently document a single "best" reply per partner, and the support gain comes mainly from triggering the scene rather than from a specific line. The only firmly documented caution here is: ⛔ during the surrounding Ball/Sothis dialogue, AVOID Sothis "I have no reason to" (Support DOWN). Treat individual Goddess-Tower replies as flavor unless a per-character source confirms otherwise — pick the warm/kind option.
